TCG Metagame Report Q1 2019

Welcome to our first TCG Metagame Report, as a new format approaches in May with a banlist and Dark Neostorm we look back at the highlights so far this year. We cover YCS Chicago, YCS Düsseldorf, YCS Atlanta and YCS Guatemala and the top 100 decks of the first quarter of 2019.

Sky Striker

Paulo Prrj Gonçalves joins the Hall of Fame with names such as Patrick Hoban as he becomes the first person to win three consecutive premiere events with his Sky Striker strategy. Beginning his streak with the 3v3 at YCS Atlanta, winning UDS Medellin the next weekend and YCS Guatemala to top it all off.

He controversially does not play Sky Striker Maneuver - Jamming Waves because it cannot destroy Sky Striker Mecha Modules – Multirole - the key card in the mirror match. He also opts for Effect Veiler in the main deck over Infinite Impermanence because of it’s ability to be kept in hand and resolve Sky Striker Mecha Modules – Multirole for more value in the end phase.

His deck profile for all 3 events explaining his choices can be found here

With Sky Striker Ace - Kagari being hit to 1 copy on the ban list it will be interesting to see if Sky Striker can win another event. It does receive some indirect support with Mystic Mine being released in Dark Neostorm.


Salamangreat

Jonas Koschel has quickly proven his place among Salamangreat duellists topping more events than anyone else in this Quarter. He notably got second place at YCS Düsseldorf, Won Cardmarket Series and topped countless Regionals. He has worked closely with his fellow E-Man Games team mate Joshua Schmidt to find the best build for this brand new strategy.

Jonas played Pot of Desires at YCS Düsseldorf then changed to Flame Bufferlo as the format progressed not wishing to lose his valuable resources played at only 1 copy such as Salamangreat Jack Jaguar, Salamangreat Roar and Salamangreat Sanctury.

Salamangreat received a small consistency hit with Lady Debug going to 1 copy on the forbidden and limited list however with Cynet Mining being released in Dark Neostorm we should still see plenty of this deck to come, it may even win it’s first event.


Thunder Dragon

Jesse Kotton has performed the best on the Thunder Dragon strategy this quarter, leading his team to an impressive second place at YCS Atlanta all piloting the 44 card Thunder Dragon deck he constructed. He also got second place at YCS Sydney and Top 16 at YCS Guatemala.

He introduced Defrag Dragon which has since become a Thunder Dragon staple for it's ability to extend through hand traps.

Thunder Dragon received no engine ban list hits but did play Summon Sorceress and Number 95: Galaxy-Eyes Dark Matter Dragon which have been forbidden also Phantom Sky Blaster which has been limited.

Raphel Neven controversially stated that YCS Quito will be the last premiere event Thunder Dragon tops.
